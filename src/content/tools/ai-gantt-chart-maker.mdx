## What is a Gantt chart?

A Gantt chart is a horizontal bar chart that shows project tasks on the vertical axis and time on the horizontal axis. Each bar represents a task, and the length and position of the bar shows when the task starts, how long it runs, and how it overlaps with other work. Milestones are typically shown as single dates rather than bars.

Gantt charts are one of the most widely used planning tools because they answer three questions in a single view: what has to happen, when it happens, and what has to wait for something else. That makes them useful from first-pass planning all the way through execution tracking.

## Who uses Gantt charts?

- Project managers coordinating cross-functional teams across a fixed deadline
- Engineering leads planning sprint sequences and dependency-heavy integration work
- Founders mapping out an MVP build, fundraise, or go-to-market schedule
- Program managers rolling up multiple workstreams into a single executive view
- Product managers aligning design, engineering, and marketing around a launch date
- Agency and consulting teams sharing timelines with clients and stakeholders
- Operations leads scheduling migrations, rollouts, and seasonal campaigns

## FAQ

### Can the AI schedule real dates, not just durations?

Yes. If your prompt names a start date or a deadline, the AI will anchor the chart to real dates. If you only give a duration, it will use a generic timeline you can shift by editing the start date in the Mermaid source.

### How do I show dependencies and overlaps?

Mention them in the prompt. Phrases like "QA starts halfway through frontend" or "integration depends on backend setup" give the AI enough signal to order tasks correctly. You can also adjust the Mermaid source directly to fine-tune start dates and dependencies after generation.
