## What is a flowchart?

A flowchart is a visual representation of a process, workflow, or decision path. It uses standardized shapes such as rectangles for steps, diamonds for decisions, and arrows for the direction of flow to show how work moves from start to finish.

Flowcharts turn abstract procedures into something you can read at a glance. They are one of the oldest and most widely used diagram types in software, operations, and business analysis because they make logic, branching, and handoffs easy to follow.

## Who uses flowcharts?

- Software engineers mapping application logic, login flows, and API behavior
- Product managers documenting user onboarding and feature decisions
- Operations and business analysts modeling internal processes
- Customer support teams outlining troubleshooting and escalation paths
- Educators and students explaining algorithms or concepts step by step
- QA engineers planning test scenarios and edge cases
- Founders and consultants communicating workflows to stakeholders
