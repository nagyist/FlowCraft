## What is a mind map?

A mind map is a radial diagram that organizes ideas around a central topic. Each branch represents a related concept, and each branch can fan out into sub-branches for more detail. The structure mirrors the way people naturally associate ideas, which makes it a good tool for brainstorming, note taking, and planning.

Mind maps work well when you need to explore a topic before committing to a linear outline. Instead of forcing ideas into a list, you can expand any branch as far as it needs to go and keep the overall shape of the topic visible at a glance.

## Who uses mind maps?

- Product managers mapping feature ideas, user needs, and open questions before a planning cycle
- Founders outlining a business plan with branches for product, market, team, and funding
- Students and researchers organizing notes around a lecture topic or literature review
- UX designers exploring user problems, personas, and feature hypotheses in the discovery phase
- Marketers brainstorming campaigns, audiences, and channels for a launch
- Writers planning articles, essays, or book chapters around a central theme
- Teachers and facilitators running group brainstorms and collecting ideas around a prompt

## Why generate mind maps with AI?

Mind maps are easy to read but slow to draw by hand. Getting the layout, spacing, and branch order right in a drawing tool takes time that would be better spent actually thinking about the topic. Typing out Mermaid syntax is faster but still requires you to hold the structure in your head while you type it.

Generating a mind map with AI removes that friction. You describe the central topic and the branches you care about in plain English, and the tool returns a structured mind map you can edit, export, or share. If the first draft misses a branch or goes too deep on one area, you can refine the prompt and regenerate rather than moving nodes around by hand.

The result is a faster cycle between rough idea and clean diagram. You get a mind map that is easy to skim and easy to hand off, without spending the meeting or study session fighting the tool.
