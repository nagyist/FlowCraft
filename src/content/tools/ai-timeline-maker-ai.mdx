## What is a timeline?

A timeline is a diagram that places events in chronological order along a single axis. Each entry is anchored to a date or period and typically carries a short label describing what happened, so readers can scan the sequence quickly.

Timelines are most useful when order and spacing matter. Whether you are documenting the history of a company, the milestones of a product, or the phases of a research project, a timeline lets you show how events relate to one another in time without forcing the reader to piece that together from prose.

## Who uses timelines?

- Students and teachers explaining historical events or scientific discoveries
- Product managers mapping release milestones and roadmap phases
- Project leads summarizing key deadlines and dependencies for stakeholders
- Writers and journalists framing the chronology of a story or investigation
- Marketers creating brand or campaign timelines for case studies
- Researchers documenting the evolution of a field, technology, or idea
- Founders telling their company story in pitch decks and About pages

## FAQ

### Can I make both historical and project timelines?

Yes. The timeline generator works for any chronological subject. Historical timelines, product roadmaps, personal career timelines, research milestones, and campaign schedules are all fair game. Just describe the time range and the events that matter.

### How detailed can the timeline be?

You can include as many events as your description covers. For readability, most effective timelines hold between five and fifteen events. If you have more, consider splitting the subject into multiple timelines or grouping events into phases.
