## What is a user journey map?

A user journey map is a visual summary of the steps a person takes to accomplish a goal with a product or service. Each step is labeled with what the user is doing and usually with how they feel at that moment, from frustrated to delighted. The result is a single picture that shows where the experience works and where it breaks down.

Journey maps differ from flowcharts because they focus on the human side of a process. They care less about the internal logic of the system and more about the emotional highs and lows a real user moves through from first touch to final outcome.

## Who uses user journey maps?

- UX researchers turning interview findings into a shared artifact the whole team can reference
- Product managers aligning engineering, design, and support on where to invest next
- Customer experience teams diagnosing drop-off points in onboarding, checkout, or renewal
- Founders stress-testing a new product idea by walking through the first-time user experience
- Service designers mapping cross-channel flows that span web, mobile, email, and human agents
- Marketers planning lifecycle campaigns that match the emotional state at each step
- Support leads identifying the moments where customers are most likely to reach out for help

## Why generate user journey maps with AI?

Journey maps are high-value artifacts, but the cost of producing them often keeps teams from using them regularly. Pulling a workshop together, transcribing sticky notes, and translating the output into a clean diagram can take a full day before the first draft exists.

Generating a journey map with AI compresses that loop. You describe the persona, the goal, and the rough stages in plain English, and the tool returns a structured map with steps and emotional scores in Mermaid-compatible syntax. You can then edit the text directly or refine the prompt until the map reflects what your research actually showed.

This shifts the bottleneck from diagram production back to thinking. Instead of spending most of your time formatting, you spend it questioning whether the stages are right, whether the emotions match the data, and what the map implies for the roadmap.

## FAQ

### How detailed should my prompt be?

Include the persona, the end-to-end goal, the key stages, and whether you want emotional states attached to each step. A prompt like "a first-time customer of an online grocery service, from discovery to first delivery, with emotions at each step" gives the AI enough to produce a useful first draft you can then refine.

### Can I use the output in presentations and documents?

Yes. You can export any journey map as SVG for crisp scaling in design tools or as PNG for quick drops into slide decks and docs. Public share links also work well for linking from a research report or a Notion page.

### Does the tool support multiple actors or swimlanes?

The native user journey format focuses on a single persona across stages with emotional scores. If you need multiple actors or swimlanes, FlowCraft also supports flowcharts and sequence diagrams, which handle that structure more naturally while still being generated from a plain English prompt.
